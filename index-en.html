Maite Mérida is a journalist dedicated to photojournalism, experimental photographic processes, collage and use of archives. The themes that give substance to her work are women's movements, feminisms, territorial struggles, affective and political memories.

She has a Diploma from the Universidad de Chile in Memories, social movements and artistic-cultural production in Chile and the Southern Cone; Photography and Contemporary Art and did a Postgraduate in Cultural Journalism, Criticism and Book Publishing. She participated in a Gender Narrative Photography residency with War Mi Photo Collective, Bolivia. In 2021, she self-published her first photochemical book. She has independently delved into workshops and seminars about photography.

Maite was granted a scholarship to study Creative Visual Storytelling in StrudelMediaLive, an artist Residency Photo Trouvée Magazine, a scholarship of photojournalism with Laboratorio de Periodismo Situado, Argentina, and a Diploma about Microcuratorships, curatorships from the marginality by the SACO Cultural Corporation.She has independently delved into workshops, seminars and residencies around photography, contemporary art, experimental video and found footage with artists, photographers and image creators from Chile, Argentina, Bolivia, Peru, Colombia, Mexico, Spain, United States, among others.

Some of her works have been part of local and international photography festivals, publications and visual arts exhibitions such as Alternative Processes: Other Ways of Seeing, NY; Asimetria Festival 17, Arequipa; Rotterdam Photo Festival, Holland and the Tiradentes Photography Festival in Brazil.
